I aim to examine how attitudes towards gay people have changed over time by comparing wave 6 and wave 5 of the world values study.

  1. The questions I am comparing ask (in both waves) “On this list are various groups of people. Can you please mention any that you would not like to have as neighbors?” I aim to compare the change in the percent of respondents who listed homosexuals as one of the groups they would not like to live near between wave 5 and wave 6 of the world values survey.

  2. I aim to show three main things. Which countries became more tolerant, which countries became less tolerant and how the countries compare in overall levels of support.

For ease of understanding I want to transform the variable so it is percent of people who do not mind having gay neighbors.

I envision the graph being a modified bar plot - showing a point for the wave 5 level of support and a point for the wave 6 level of support (for all countries). I want to order the countries in order of supportiveness so that the order of the axis is conveying additional information.

Followng Tufte’s rules on non data ink, I want to include minimal ink to convey the data as clearly as possible. So will instead of a bar plot use dots for the wave 5 and wave 6 results for each country, and perhaps connect the dots with a line so that it is easier for readers to compare the change over time between countries.

  1. Eventually the Data frame will be of the form {“Country”,“Wave Number”,“Percent Supporting”}

This is how I originally thought the data frame would look. Eventaully due to the aesthetics I descided to use (ie arrows showing both size and direction of change in attitudes in two different graphs) the data ended up being in two different data frames (separate data frames for positve change and for negative change) each in the form {“Country”,“Approval in wave 5”,“Approval in Wave 6”}
